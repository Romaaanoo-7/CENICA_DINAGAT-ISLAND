About Dinagat Islands

The Mystical Province of Love

Discover the rich history, vibrant culture, and mesmerizing landscapes of Dinagat Islands.

Welcome to
Dinagat Islands
The Hidden Gem

Dinagat Islands is a province in the Caraga region of the Philippines, nestled on the south side of Leyte Gulf. Known for its karst landscapes, untouched beaches, and mystical aura, it became an independent province in 2006.


The province is steeped in history and culture. It is famously known as the headquarters of the Philippine Benevolent Missionaries Association (PBMA), giving the island a unique spiritual significance among locals. The towering Shrine of Ruben Ecleo Sr. in San Jose stands as an iconic landmark overlooking the town.


Beyond its cultural intrigue, Dinagat is endowed with immense natural wealth. From the fascinating underwater caves of Lake Bababu to the pristine shores of Bitaog Beach and the tidal pools of Pangabangan Island, it is a paradise waiting to be explored by nature lovers and adventurers alike.
